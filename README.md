**Training as World-Building: How Training Data Constructs AI Ontology**

`PROJECT STATUS: RESEARCH PHASE`

## Overview

Just as the Architect's design choices shaped the Matrix's reality, training data choices shape an LLM's "world." This project systematically maps how training data composition creates implicit ontological commitments in models — what exists in the model's world, what doesn't, and what's distorted.

## Research Question

How does the composition of training data construct the model's ontology (what it thinks exists), epistemology (what it thinks it knows), and axiology (what it thinks matters)?

## Methodology

### 1. Cross-Model Comparison
Compare models trained on different data distributions:
- English-only vs multilingual: does the model's "world" change?
- Pre-2020 vs post-2020 data: what exists in one world but not another?
- Science-heavy vs fiction-heavy training: different ontologies?

### 2. Ontological Probes
Questions that reveal implicit worldview:
- What does the model think exists? (Probe rare, culturally specific concepts)
- What does the model think is important? (Test implicit value hierarchies)
- What does the model think is impossible? (Probe near-possible innovations)

### 3. Ontological Fingerprinting
- Map the unique "ontological fingerprint" of each model
- Test: can models identify their own ontological biases when prompted?

### 4. The Vedantic Connection
**Ishvara** (the cosmic architect) — training data as the creator's blueprint. The data that builds an AI's world is analogous to the cosmic intelligence that structures perceived reality.

### 5. Implications for AI Fairness
Training data ontology = whose reality gets encoded. This has direct implications for representation, bias, and the politics of AI world-building.

## Expected Outputs

- **Paper:** *"The Architect's Blueprint: How Training Data Constructs AI Ontology and Reality"*
- **Visualization:** Interactive "ontological map" comparing world models across LLMs
- **Blog series:** *"Who Built Your AI's Reality?"*
